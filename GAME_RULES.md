**Extended, detailed version of Tunisian Rummy (Rami Tounsi / Kbābet)**.
This is the **most commonly played Tunisian version**. Minor house rules exist, but this is the reference one.

# Tunisian Rummy (Rami / Kbābet) — Full Rules

## 1. Objective

Be the **first player to get rid of all cards** by forming **valid melds**:

- **Runs (Sequences)**
- **Sets (Groups)**

## 2. Number of Players

- **4 players**

## 3. Cards Used

- **2 standard 52-card decks**
- **+ 2 Jokers per deck**
  Total: **108 cards**

## 4. Card Values (for scoring)

| Card      | Points                                    |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Ace (A)   | 1 point                                   |
| 2 – 10    | Face value                                |
| Jack (J)  | 10 points                                 |
| Queen (Q) | 10 points                                 |
| King (K)  | 10 points                                 |
| Joker     | 20–25 points (house rule, usually **25**) |

Ace can be:

- **Low** (A-2-3)
- **High** (Q-K-A)

---

## 5. Dealing

- Each player gets **14 cards** except first player who gets **15** cards
- Remaining cards form the **draw pile**
- first player starts by discarding one card in the
  **discard pile**
- the second player draws from the **draw pile**
- the game continues

## 6. Valid Melds

### A) Runs (Silsila)

- **Minimum 3 cards**
- Same **suit**
- Consecutive order

Examples:

- 3:hearts: 4:hearts: 5:hearts:
- 7:spades: 8:spades: 9:spades: 10:spades:
- A:diamonds: 2:diamonds: 3:diamonds:
- Q:clubs: K:clubs: A:clubs:

Invalid:

- Mixed suits

### B) Sets (Jmou3)

- **3 or 4 cards**
- Same **rank**
- Different suits

:white_check_mark: Examples:

- 7:clubs: 7:diamonds: 7:hearts:
- K:spades: K:clubs: K:diamonds: K:hearts:

:x: Invalid:

- Same suit repeated
- Two cards only

---

## 7. Jokers (Joker)

Jokers are **wild cards**.

### Joker Rules:

- Can replace **any card**
- Cannot stand alone
- Must be part of a valid meld

Example:

- 5:spades: 6:spades: Joker → counts as 7:spades:

### Joker Replacement Rule (Very Important)

If a meld is on the table:

- A player **can replace a Joker** with the real card
- The Joker goes into the replacing player’s hand
- Joker **must be reused immediately** in another meld

## 8. Game Flow (Turn Order)

Each turn:

1. **Draw**

   - Take **1 card** from:

     - Draw pile OR
     - Top of discard pile

2. **Meld (optional)**

   - Place new melds
   - Add cards to existing melds
   - Replace Jokers (if possible)

3. **Discard**

   - Discard **1 card** to end turn

## 9. Opening (First Meld Requirement)

Before placing anything on the table, a player must **open**.

### Opening Condition:

- Melds totaling **at least 71 points**
- All melds must be valid
- Jokers allowed

If you don’t reach 71:

- You cannot put anything on the table
- You must wait

Once opened:

- You can add cards freely in later turns

---

## 10. Adding to Existing Melds

After opening, you may:

- Extend runs (add before or after)
- Add cards to sets (up to 5 for runs and up to 4 for sets)

Example:

- Run: 4:hearts: 5:hearts: 6:hearts: → add 7:hearts:
- Set: 9:clubs: 9:diamonds: 9:hearts: → add 9:spades: then set is closed

## 11. Going Out (Winning a Round)

A player wins the round by:

- Getting rid of **all cards**
- Discarding the final card
- Final card **can be a Joker**

## 12. Scoring (For Multi-Round Games)

After someone wins:

- All other players **count cards in hand**
- Points are **added to their score**

Game ends when:

- Someone reaches **700 points**

Winner = **lowest total score**

## 13. Special Rules & Variations (Common in Tunisia)

### A) Joker Limit

- Some tables allow **only 1 Joker per meld**
- Others allow unlimited

### B) No Joker Finish

- You cannot finish the game using a Joker as last card

### C) Forced Discard

- Even if you finish melds, you **must discard** unless stated otherwise

### D) Stealing Joker

- Mandatory reuse immediately
- No keeping Joker in hand

---

## 14. Strategy Tips (Real Tunisian Style)

- Don’t rush opening unless strong
- Track Jokers carefully
- Keep middle cards (5–9) longer
- High cards early = danger
- Blocking discard pile is key
